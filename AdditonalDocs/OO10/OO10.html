Organizational Overview 10
Exemplary Professional Practice

 

Blank templates are required for nurses at all levels (staff nurses up to and including the CNO):

  • Self-appraisal tools
  • Peer feedback tools
  • Performance review tools

 

 

Nurse leaders per ANCC definition include Nursing Directors, Asst. Vice Presidents, and Vice Presidents of Nursing at Texas Children’s Hospital.  Positons that do not fall under ANCC nurse leader definition, but are addressed here to encompass “all levels” are PCMs, ACDs, Nurse Educators, CNS, and APPs, and will not be found on the leadership table under nurse leader. Please see below revised description to clarify all tools utilized by all levels. 

 

 

Nurses at all levels at Texas Children’s Hospital (TCH) complete a self-appraisal tool, a peer feedback tool, and a performance review tool annually. 

 

TCH utilizes the organization-wide policy to guide the procedures related to the self-appraisal and performance review tool process (Evidence OO10-1, Performance Management Policy). The tool consists of objectives, success profiles, and development plan. The functionality of the electronic document does not allow printing of blank tools for some steps of the performance evaluation process; blinded, completed tools have therefore been submitted in some instances.

 

Peer feedback tools are completed per Nursing Department guidelines.  The appropriate peer feedback tool is selected in the electronic system depending on the nurse’s role.  The individual selects 2-3 nursing peers to receive the role specific peer feedback from.  The supervisor is able to select 2-3 additional nursing peers (dependent on the department size).  After feedback is completed, the employee receives a summary of numeric scores and verbatim comments. 

 

Below is a description of each review process:

 

Clinical Nurses:

  • Clinical nurses’ self-appraisal, peer feedback tool, and performance review tools are submitted annually.
  • There is an in-patient and ambulatory template for clinical nurses to select, depending on the nurses’ clinical area.
  • Clinical nurses from all settings utilize the same peer feedback tool (regardless of location).
    • (Evidence OO10-2, Inpatient Clinical Nurse Self-Appraisal Tool) 
    • (Evidence OO10-3, Inpatient Clinical Nurse Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-4, Ambulatory Clinical Nurse Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-5, Ambulatory Clinical Nurse Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-6, Clinical Nurse Peer Feedback)

 

Advanced Practice Provider:

  • Advanced Practice Providers’ self-appraisal, peer feedback tool, and performance review tools are submitted annually.
  • Nurse Practitioners and Clinical Nurse Specialists both fall within this category, and each have templates to complete the self-appraisal, peer feedback tool, and performance review tools, depending on their role.
  • Nurse Practitioners use the Focused Professional Practice Evaluation (FPPE) and Ongoing Professional Practice Evaluation (OPPE) form which serves as their peer feedback tool.  This tool is filled out every 6 months by a selection of 2-3 peers.
  • Clinical Nurse Specialists have a peer feedback tool, completed by nursing peers annually.
    • (Evidence OO10-7, Advanced Practice Provider Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-8, Advanced Practice Provider Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-9, Advanced Practice Provider Peer Feedback Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-10, Clinical Specialist Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-11, Clinical Specialist Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-12, Clinical Specialist Peer Feedback Tool)

 

Nurse Educator:

  • Nurse educators’ self-appraisal, peer feedback tool, and performance review tools are submitted annually. 
  • Nurse educators have centralized objectives and success profiles that align with the nursing strategic plan. The tool is individualized to meet the specific clinical area’s quality, safety, and operational goals.
  • Ambulatory nurse educators have centralized objectives and success profiles that align with the nursing strategic plan. The tool is individualized to meet the specific quality, safety, and operational goals in the Ambulatory setting.
  • All nurse educators utilize the same peer feedback tool.
    • (Evidence OO10-13, Inpatient Education Coordinator Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-14, Inpatient Education Coordinator Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-15, Ambulatory Education Coordinator Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-16, Ambulatory Education Coordinator Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-17, Nurse Educator Peer Feedback Tool)

 

Nurse Manager:

  • Nurse managers’ self-appraisal, peer feedback tool, and performance review tools are submitted annually. 
  • Nurse managers have centralized objectives and success profiles that align with the nursing strategic plan. The tool is individualized to meet the specific clinical area’s quality, safety, and operational goals.
  • The Inpatient Patient Care Manager, Ambulatory Patient Care Manager, and Assistant Clinical Director roles all fall under the Magnet definition of nurse manager.  All of these roles have different core centralized objectives and success profiles.  A copy of all three templates have been included for review.
  • All nurse manager roles utilize the nurse manager peer feedback tool.  Each role identifies nursing peers at their level within the organization to receive feedback from. 
    • (Evidence OO10-18, Inpatient Patient Care Manager Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-19, Inpatient Patient Care Manager Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-20, Ambulatory Patient Care Manager Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-21, Ambulatory Patient Care Manager Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-22, Assistant Clinical Director Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-23, Assistant Clinical Director Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-24, Nurse Manager Peer Feedback Tool)

 

Nurse Leader (Nursing Directors, Asst. Vice Presidents of Nursing, and Vice Presidents of Nursing):

  • Nurse leaders’ self-appraisal, peer feedback tool, and performance review tools are submitted annually. 
  • Nursing Directors, Asst. Vice Presidents of Nursing, and Vice Presidents of Nursing all fall under the Magnet definition of nurse leader, and utilize different self-appraisal and performance review tools.
  • Nursing Directors have centralized objectives and success profiles that align with the nursing strategic plan. The tool is individualized to meet the specific clinical area’s quality, safety, and operational goals.
  • Asst. Vice Presidents and Vice Presidents of Nursing are considered executive leadership at TCH.  The Texas Children's Executive Success Profile was leveraged to create a leadership competency model which differentiates outstanding executive leadership performance. In addition, the CNO is able to incorporate aspects of her strategic plan in the performance review to assure the nursing executives are aligned and making progress on the strategic initiatives for nursing.  Asst. Vice Presidents and Vice Presidents of Nursing self-appraisal and performance review tools are contained in the same document.  The Asst. Vice Presidents and Vice Presidents of Nursing fill out the “areas of strength” and “opportunities for development” columns as a method of self-appraisal.  Next, their supervisor validates these two columns as a performance review.  Finally, the Asst. Vice Presidents and Vice Presidents of Nursing and their supervisor work collaboratively to develop items to complete the “action items” column.
  • The Directors of Nursing, Asst. Vice President, and Vice Presidents of Nursing utilize the same Nurse Leader Peer Feedback tool.  Each role identifies nursing peers at their level within the organization to receive feedback from. 
    • (Evidence OO10-25 Director Self-Appraisal Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-26, Director Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-27, Asst. VP and VP Nursing Self-Appraisal & Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-28, Nurse Leader  Peer Feedback Tool) - used by Nursing Director, Asst. Vice President and Vice President

 

Chief Nursing Officer:

  • The Chief Nursing Officer (CNO) self-appraisal, peer feedback tool, and performance review tools are submitted annually.
  • The Texas Children's Executive Success Profile was leveraged to create a leadership competency model which differentiates outstanding executive leadership performance. In addition, the CNO is able to incorporate aspects of her strategic plan in the performance review to assure the nursing executives are aligned and making progress on the strategic initiatives for nursing.
  • The CNO self-appraisal and performance review tools are contained in the same document.  The CNO fills out the “areas of strength” and “opportunities for development” columns as a method of self-appraisal.  Next, the CNO’s supervisor (the executive vice president) and the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) validates these two columns as a performance review.  Finally, the CNO and the CEO work collaboratively to develop items to complete the “action items” column.
  • The CNO participates in peer feedback annually.  Peer feedback is obtained from other executives at her level, who are also nurses within the organization.
    • (Evidence OO10-29, CNO Self-Appraisal & Performance Review Tool)
    • (Evidence OO10-30, CNO Peer Feedback Tool)
