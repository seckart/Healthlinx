2015-2016 Annual Learning Needs Assessment (ALNA)

 

Overall Description
At Texas Children’s Hospital (TCH), the Annual Learning Needs Assessment (ALNA) is used to identify and track the self-identified educational needs of nurses at all levels from chief nurse officer to clinical nurses in all inpatient and ambulatory settings. It is an organized method for identifying the needs of nurses at all levels to increase competency and improve patient care in the inpatient and ambulatory settings.

 

Description of All Settings
Texas Children’s Hospital consists of an inpatient and ambulatory setting. There are thirty-two inpatient units, one hundred and seven sub-specialty ambulatory clinics, two emergency centers, eight operating room locations, and five health centers.

 

ALNA Survey Design
The Nursing Professional Development Department is committed to the learning needs of all nurses, in all settings, through the development and implementation of the ALNA.  Each year, nursing professional development specialists meet with representatives from nursing and key stakeholders to develop and design the ALNA survey. In June 2015, the ALNA Advisory Workgroup which comprised of clinical nurses, nurse educators, nurse managers, nurse leaders, and business system analysts, was developed to provide direction for the ALNA Process. Sharon Demel, MSN, CNS, RNC-NIC, Nursing Professional Development Specialist, led the ALNA initiative and chaired the ALNA Advisory Workgroup. The surveys were designed using the Nurse Executive Board Certification Exam, American Organization of Nurse Executive (AONE) Assessment Tool, professional and organizational information based on roles, pathways, risk management report, evidence-based practice (EBP), department initiatives and organizational initiatives. The ALNA Advisory Workgroup solicited and received additional input from content experts within the respective survey groups as needed to complete and validate the items. The ALNA Advisory Workgroup developed the types and structures of the surveys for each level and setting, including inpatient and ambulatory. The ALNA survey types include:

  • Chief Nurse Officer (CNO)
  • Nurse Leaders (Vice Presidents of Nursing, Assistant Vice Presidents of Nursing  and Director of Nursing)
  • Nurse Managers
  • Nurse Educators
  • Clinical Nurses
  • Case Manager/Care Manager
  • Advanced Practice Registered Nurses (APRNs)
  • Patient Care Assistants and Medical Assistants

 

2015 Annual Learning Needs Assessment Survey Description
The surveys were created by the ALNA Advisory Workgroup with SurveyMonkey for the CNO, nurse leaders, nurse managers, nurse educators, clinical nurses, care managers/case managers, advanced practice registered nurses, patient care assistants and medical assistants in all inpatient and ambulatory settings. The ALNA surveys were assigned through the Required Training Track by Yaima Aleman, MBA, BS, Nursing Professional Department Specialist. Within each survey, respondents selected their roles, setting and department. The surveys were administered from October 29, 2015 through March 2016.

 

Five surveys were used for nurses in all inpatient and ambulatory settings:

Development of Implementation Plan for all Levels and all Settings
SurveyMonkey data analysis tools were used by the ALNA Advisory Workgroup to filter results by role category, and the following results were analyzed:

  • Top 3 topics for each survey category
  • Top 3 ways of receiving information
  • Topics for future continuing nursing education planning

 

Educational needs at all levels and settings were reviewed, validated, and plans were led by the ALNA Advisory Workgroup in collaboration from representatives in each level, (CNO, nurse leaders, nurse managers, nurse educators, clinical nurses, care managers/case managers, advanced practice registered nurses, patient care assistants and medical assistants). The identified needs were prioritized by ranking the number of responses for each topic in each of the major groups. The implementation plan for each level, aligns with the nursing strategic priorities and incorporates the inpatient and ambulatory settings. The ALNA Advisory Workgroup led the implementation plan, in collaboration with each level in all settings, by identifying and utilizing new and existing internal and external resources. Advanced Practice Providers Implementation of the Education Plan

The implementation plan for the advanced practice providers (APP), inpatient and ambulatory setting, was created by the Advanced Practice Provider Annual Conference Planning Committee, which is comprised of the APPs Director of Nursing and APPs in the inpatient and ambulatory setting. The planning committee for this internally created and publicly available conference follows the continuing nursing education (CNE) planning process of the TCH ANCC/TNA Provider Unit. The planning committee reviewed the ALNA survey results, prioritized the needs identified within the survey, and aligned them with nursing strategic priorities. The Advanced Practice Provider Annual Conference Planning Committee for the annual Perinatal Outreach Program, whose target audience is TCH and other external advance practice providers, also uses the ALNA survey results for their conference planning.

Patient Care Assistants (PCA) and Medical Assistants (MA)
Implementation of the Education Plan

The ALNA Advisory Workgroup led the implementation of the patient care assistants (Inpatient) and medical assistants (Ambulatory) education plan in collaboration with Patient Care Assistants (PCA) Pathway to Success Program Planning Committee from the inpatient and ambulatory settings. The Patient Care Assistants (PCA) Pathway to Success Program Planning Committee was comprised of patient care assistants, medical assistants, nurse educators, nurse managers, nurse leaders, and a Nursing Professional Development Department specialist. This steering committee reviewed the ALNA survey results, and incorporated other feedback elicited from orientation effectiveness surveys from patient care assistants and medical assistants, and other feedback from the nurse leaders and nurse managers.  An implementation plan was established based on a prioritization of the top continuing education needs. Based on this work, the Patient Care Assistants (PCA) Pathway to Success Program Planning Committee, in collaboration with the ALNA Advisory Workgroup, created the 12 hour curriculum that was offered January 2016 through July 2016 for current patient care assistants and medical assistants to meet the identified needs. The 12 hour curriculum was also offered monthly as needed thereafter for new patient care assistants and medical assistants’ hires.
