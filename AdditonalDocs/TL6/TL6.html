Advocacy and Influence TL6

Example Three: Mentoring Activities for Ketrese White – Nurse Leader

 

Texas Children’s Hospital’s (TCH) Chief Nursing Officer Mary Jo Andre, MSN, RN, NEA-BC, recognizes the importance of developing nursing leaders internally through mentoring activities to be successful in their new nurse leadership role and to fill key leadership positions within TCH when expected or unexpected departures occur. Andre invests in nurse leaders and provides exposure and networking for continued growth.

 

Ketrese White, MHA, RN, NE-BC, Director of Patient Care Services, began her career at TCH more than 17 years ago as a staff nurse in the Cancer/Hematology Centers on the Bone Marrow Transplant (BMT) unit, where she also performed charge nurse and preceptor roles. White moved into nurse leadership positions (nurse educator, then nurse manager) while on the BMT unit. In 2010, White advanced to Assistant Director of Inpatient Services, Child Life, and the Cancer and Hematology clinic & Infusion Center at TCH West Campus, where she exhibited great leadership in the opening of the first community-based dedicated pediatric hospital in the Houston area. White defined the patient experience process, assisted in the development of strategies and business plans, established recruitment and hiring practices, staffing and training plans while at West Campus. (Evidence TL6-12, Nurse Leader Ketrese White Resume)

 

In 2015, TCH began its newest expansion of Texas Children’s Hospital - The Woodlands, which is north of Houston. Michelle Riley-Brown, FACHE, President for Texas Children’s Hospital - The Woodlands, recognized White’s leadership potential. In June 2015, as plans for the expansion were underway, White was named Director of Nursing for Patient Care Services TCH - The Woodlands. In collaboration with Riley-Brown, Andre identified ongoing mentoring activities for White which included:

  • Attending the Nursing Leadership Institute (NLI)
  • Leading The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team
  • Completion of the Leader Performance Continuum Courses
  • Providing organization exposure through leadership meetings and forums

 

Ketrese White – Mentoring Activities

 

Nursing Leadership Institute
The Nursing Leadership Institute (NLI) is an intensive program that provides mentoring activities or succession planning for nurse leaders. It was developed in 2014 by executives and Board members from the Texas Medical Center and many other hospitals in the same geographical area to provide development opportunities for career advancement. Texas Children’s Hospital (TCH) is one of the many organizations that participate. There is an annual selection process for participants, who must be recommended by a chief nursing officer. The target audience includes CNOs, aspiring CNOs, nurse leaders identified for mentorship or succession planning, and nursing faculty and leadership. The NLI gives participants an opportunity to develop leadership skills while networking with other nursing leaders from around the community.

 

NLI course topics include personal leadership development; organizational values, assessment and governance; quality measurement and improvement strategies; financial and business management; strategic planning and implementation; personnel leadership and management; managing large groups; community health, community engagement, and population health management; and innovation and information technology. Meetings for this 96-hour, multiple-session course are held monthly on a Thursday and Friday from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m.

 

White’s classes began on January 28, 2016. The NLI topics and agenda for each class were as follows:

  • 1/28/16 – Personal Leadership Development
  • 1/29/16 – Organizational Values, Assessment and Governance
  • 2/11/16 – Strategic Planning and Implementation
  • 2/12/16 – Quality Measurement and Improvement Strategies (Clinical and Customer Experience)
  • 3/31/16 – Healthcare Environment: Reform and Its Impact on the Nursing Workforce
  • 4/01/16 – Financial and Business Management – Finance 101
  • 4/28/16 – Community and Population Health Management
  • 4/29/16 – Personnel Leadership and Management
  • 5/12/16 – Personnel Leadership and Management
  • 5/13/16 – Project Presentation and Graduation

 

White graduated from the NLI, and the tools that she gained will continue to help her be successful in her leadership journey. White also developed professional relationships that will last throughout her career. She said it was an honor to be selected into the prestigious NLI, and that the entire experience made a positive impact on her growth and career. (Evidence TL6c-a, Nurse Leadership Institute Overview, Ketrese White Nurse Leadership Institute Class Agendas and Certificate from Nurse Leadership Institute)

 

Leading The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team
Andre recognized a mentoring activity for White to lead the The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team activation planning team.  Beginning 2016, White spearheaded The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team, which consisted of inter-professional team members from nursing, pharmacy, respiratory care, quality/infection control, human resources, radiology, risk management, compliance, biomedical engineering, information services, facilities operations, clinical nutrition services, and security. White developed The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team charter and the project plan in preparation for operations and patient care services with the opening of The Woodlands. The project plan for The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team included:

  • Staffing – identification, review, and modification of current staffing tools and proposed staffing plans
  • Scope of Practice – identification, modification, and approval of scope of care
  • Policies and Procedures – identification and modification of applicable policies and procedures based upon The Woodlands operations
  • Workflows and Operations – identification of current best practices for implementation and modification of select processes to meet the needs of the ideal patient experience at The Woodlands
  • Supplies, Equipment, and Forms – identification and modification of necessary supplies, equipment, and forms that will support The Woodlands operations
  • Mock Drills/Simulation – participation in mock drills to ensure adequate preparation prior to opening of The Woodlands

 

As the chair of The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team, White held bi-weekly to monthly meetings. With her direction, Lindi Stegeman, BSN, RN, CPEN, Kimberly Holt, BSN, RN, CPN, Susan Romero, MSN, RN, NE-BC, and Juanna Brandon, MBA, BSN, NE-BC, the co-chairs and members of The Woodlands leadership, facilitated some of The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team meetings.  On February 10, 2016, White reviewed TCH - The Woodlands Patient Care Services Activation Team Charter, team structure, project plan, expectations, and deliverables with The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team. At the meeting on February 24, 2016, White gained agreement from The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team on TCH - The Woodlands Patient Care Services Activation Team Charter, discussed staffing tools and the orientation timelines. From March 2016 to September 2016, White led The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team with drafting, revising, and finalizing staffing, scope of practice, policies and procedures, workflows and operations, supplies, equipment, necessary forms. The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team initiated training and testing through simulation in September 2016 for outpatient and March 2017 for inpatient. White’s leadership with The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team, led to the successful opening of the Texas Children’s Hospital - The Woodlands outpatient clinics in October 2016, and the inpatient services in April 2017. (Evidence TL6c-b, The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team Charter, Meeting Minutes, Meeting Agenda)

 

Leader Performance Continuum Courses
In May 2016, White completed the leadership continuum courses and Targeted Selection refresher course. These courses equipped White to:

  • Leverage processes and tools across the entire performance management continuum
  • Encourage people to take ownership and accountability of their work performance
  • Deliver feedback that gets results
  • Manage work performance issues in a fair and consistent manner
  • Conduct effective ongoing performance management conversations
  • Coach employees to perform at their highest potential
  • Leverage the Targeted Selection system to make informed hiring decisions (Evidence TL6c-c, Performance Continuum Course, Targeted Selection Refresher Objectives & Ketrese White Leadership Courses Transcript Report)

 

Organization Exposure through Leadership Meetings and Forums
With Andre’s direction, on April 4, 2016 Janet Austin, Senior Administrative Assistant for Andre, sent White an email to inform her that she was on the agenda at the upcoming Nursing Coordinating Council (NCC) meeting which is comprised of the chief nurse officer, nurse leaders, and the clinical nurse chair and chair-elect of each council in the shared governance model. Andre determined this as an opportunity for White to continue to develop skills set, presence, and recognition. White presented The Woodlands project update to the NCC on April 26, 2016 and received verbal recognition from Andre, nurse executives, and nurse leaders. On April 28, 2016, Riley-Brown notified White of the opportunity to present The Woodlands planning update at the Systems Leadership Forum that includes the chief executive officer, executive vice presidents, and physician-in chiefs. White presented the vision and structure of The Woodlands at the Texas Children’s System Leadership Forum in July 2016. Riley-Brown, chief executive officer, and the senior executives provided positive feedback with White’s engagement and presentation. Andre recognized other opportunities for White to develop in her leadership role. In May 2016 and September 2016, Andre informed Jody Childs, MBA, BSN, RN, Senior Project Manager, to send an email requesting White to be on the panel to speak about the planning and progress as she prepares for The Woodlands opening at the town hall meeting in June 2016 and December 2016. At the June 2016 and December 2016 town hall meeting, White provided project plan updates for The Woodlands Campus Patient Care Services Activation Team, shared a video of the progress made, and The Woodlands projected growth with clinical nurses, nurse managers, nurse leaders including nurse executives, chief nurse officer, and other inter-professional teams from across the organization. (Evidence TL6c-d, NCC - Email & Meeting Minutes, Systems Leadership Forum - Email, Meeting Minutes, Recognition Email, Town Hall - Email and White Presentations)

 

White’s mentoring activities were pivotal in her development as the Director of Nursing for The Woodlands. With Andre’s organizational support, White continues to excel as an instrumental and influential nurse leader. White accomplishments include:

  • Strategic and operational planning for patient care services
  • Leadership through construction, design, development and implementation of The Woodlands
  • Opening of The Woodlands which is Texas Children’s Hospital’s second community-based pediatric hospital
