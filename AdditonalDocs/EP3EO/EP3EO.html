EP3EO

 

Professional Practice Model EP3EO

 

Unit- or clinic-level nurse (RN) satisfaction data outperform the mean or median of the national database used. Note: Benchmark used must be one to which the organization contributes data.

  • Provide unit-based, national benchmarked nurse (RN) satisfaction data from the most recent survey administered within the previous 30 months before documentation submission.

 

Note: This SOE becomes effective April 1, 2016. Until that time, the 2008 EP3EO is in effect. 2008 Application Manual EP3EO: Nurse satisfaction or engagement data aggregated at the organizational, clinical groups of like-units or unit level outperform the mean, median, or other benchmark statistic of the national database used. Submit data for the most recent nurse satisfaction survey within the previous 30-months before documentation submission. Include participation rates, analysis, and evaluation of the data.

 

Nurse (RN) satisfaction survey must include questions related to the following seven categories. Data must be submitted on your choice of four of the seven categories.

  1. Autonomy
  2. Professional development (education, resources, etc.)
  3. Leadership access and responsiveness (includes nursing administration/CNO)
  4. Interprofessional relationships (includes all disciplines)

  5. Fundamentals of quality nursing care

  6. Adequacy of resources and staffing

  7. RN-to-RN teamwork and collaboration

 

For system applications, refer to system requirements in Appendix C.

Assessing RN Satisfaction

 

Texas Children’s Hospital (TCH) uses the Press Ganey Employee Engagement Survey that includes the ANCC-approved Magnet® Bundle to assess registered nurse (RN) satisfaction. The Press Ganey survey provides unit-based, nationally benchmarked nurse (RN) satisfaction data, which gives TCH leaders access to vital information that they use to develop strategies to improve nurse engagement.

 

Participation Rate
The most recent survey was conducted from June 13 through June 26, 2016. With a total RN pool of approximately 3,100, the participation rate was 71%. Of the total 2,210 nursing respondents, 1,731 (78%) were eligible for inclusion in the Magnet analysis. The data from the TCH survey was used in the calculation of Press Ganey’s July 2016 norms.

 

Performance
In the 2016 Press Ganey Employee Engagement Survey that includes the ANCC-approved Magnet® Bundle, 29 of 56 TCH nursing units outperformed.

 

Evaluation of Data
The Press Ganey categories of nursing satisfaction directly match the ANCC-approved categories, and they report the mean as their measure of central tendency. The four categories selected for benchmarking at TCH are:

  • Professional Development
  • Interprofessional Relationships
  • Fundamentals of Quality Nursing Care
  • Adequacy of Resources and Staffing

 

Results are provided at the unit level and benchmarked against three Press Ganey Benchmark Normative Means from July 2016:

  • National Nursing Excellence July 2016 Mean
  • National Nursing Excellence July Pediatrics 2016 Mean
  • National Nursing Excellence July Surgical 2016 Mean
